# Account Access and Authentication Guide

## Password Issues

### Forgot Password
If you've forgotten your password, you can reset it using the self-service portal:

1. Go to https://portal.example.com/login
2. Click "Forgot Password?"
3. Enter your corporate email address
4. Check your email for the reset link (arrives within 5 minutes)
5. Click the link and create a new password
6. Your password must be at least 12 characters with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols

### Password Not Working
If your password isn't working:
- Check if Caps Lock is enabled
- Verify you're using your corporate email address (not personal)
- Make sure your account hasn't been locked due to too many failed attempts
- Contact IT if locked out (we can unlock immediately)

### Account Locked
Your account locks automatically after 5 failed login attempts for security. To unlock:
- Wait 30 minutes for automatic unlock, OR
- Contact IT support for immediate unlock
- We'll verify your identity and unlock your account right away

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

### Setting Up MFA
All corporate accounts require MFA setup:
1. Download Microsoft Authenticator app on your phone
2. Log in to https://portal.example.com/security
3. Click "Set up MFA"
4. Scan the QR code with the Authenticator app
5. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm setup

### Lost MFA Device
If you lost your phone or can't access your MFA device:
- Contact IT support immediately
- We'll temporarily disable MFA for your account
- You can then set up MFA on a new device
- Security policy requires MFA re-enablement within 24 hours

### MFA Code Not Working
Common issues:
- Make sure your phone's time is correct (auto-sync enabled)
- Try generating a new code
- Check you're using the right account in Authenticator
- Use backup codes if you saved them during setup

## Access Permissions

### Need Access to Shared Drive
To request access to a shared folder or drive:
1. Note the exact folder path or name
2. Email IT with: folder name, business justification, and your manager's approval
3. We'll grant access within 2 business hours during work hours
4. Urgent requests: call IT for same-day access

### Application Access Requests
For software or application access:
- Specify the application name and version needed
- Provide business justification
- Manager approval required for licensed software
- Access typically granted within 1 business day
- Some applications require training completion first

## Common Error Messages

### "Your credentials could not be verified"
This usually means:
- Wrong username or password
- Account is locked or disabled
- Password has expired (must change every 90 days)
- Contact IT if you've checked all the above

### "Account is not authorized"
You don't have permission to access this resource. Submit an access request through the IT portal or email IT support with details of what you need access to and why.

### "Account temporarily disabled"
Your account may be disabled if:
- You haven't logged in for 90+ days
- Your employment status changed
- Security policy violation
- Contact IT immediately to reactivate

## Security Best Practices

### Password Security
- Never share your password with anyone, including IT staff
- Don't reuse passwords from other websites
- Change your password if you suspect it's been compromised
- Use a password manager to remember complex passwords

### Suspicious Activity
Report to IT immediately if you notice:
- Unexpected password reset emails you didn't request
- Login notifications from locations you weren't at
- Unfamiliar devices showing in your account settings
- Files or emails you didn't create or send
