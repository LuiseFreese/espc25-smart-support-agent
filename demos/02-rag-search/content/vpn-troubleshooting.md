# VPN Connection Guide

## Connecting to VPN

To connect to the company VPN:

1. Open the VPN client application
2. Enter your credentials (same as portal login)
3. Select the appropriate server:
   - **US-East** for users in Americas
   - **EU-West** for users in Europe
   - **AP-Southeast** for users in Asia-Pacific
4. Click "Connect"
5. Wait for connection confirmation (green checkmark)

## Common VPN Issues

### VPN Disconnects Frequently

If your VPN connection drops every few minutes:

1. **Check your internet connection**: Ensure stable connectivity
2. **Update VPN client**: Download latest version from IT portal
3. **Change protocols**: Switch from UDP to TCP in settings
4. **Adjust MTU size**: Set to 1400 in advanced settings
5. **Disable power saving**: Prevent network adapter from sleeping

### Cannot Connect to VPN

If you cannot establish a VPN connection:

1. Verify your credentials are correct
2. Check if your account is active (contact HR if suspended)
3. Ensure firewall allows VPN traffic (ports 443, 1194)
4. Try a different VPN server location
5. Restart your computer and try again

### Slow VPN Performance

To improve VPN speed:

1. Connect to the nearest server geographically
2. Close bandwidth-intensive applications
3. Use wired connection instead of Wi-Fi
4. Check for ISP throttling
5. Contact IT if consistently slow

## VPN Best Practices

- Always connect to VPN when accessing company resources
- Disconnect when not in use to free up licenses
- Never share VPN credentials
- Report suspicious connection attempts immediately
- Update VPN client monthly
